This document is the report of my internship at KU Leuven’s Quantitative Psychology and Individual Differences Research Group in the academic year 2018-2019.

I start Chapter 2 with an introduction and elaborate the research questions, methods, research material, and a short overwies of the following chapters. I also discuss how the goal of the internship evolved over time and how it differed from the initial intentions. Then I move on to Chapter 3 the first round of analyses, namely principal component analysis (PCA), and report the results. Later in Chapter @(irt) I introduce item response theory (IRT) and discuss the outcomes of the premilinary explorations via IRT.
